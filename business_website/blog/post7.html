Building a Winning Team

Published August 14, 2025

Your people are your greatest asset. A skilled, motivated team not only delivers outstanding work but also represents your brand to clients and drives growth through positive interactions and referrals. However, hiring and retaining top talent in the trades is a challenge many service businesses face. This article shares strategies for attracting, training and keeping a winning team.

Define Your Culture and Values

Start by clarifying the kind of company you want to build. What values matter most? Do you emphasise safety, craftsmanship, punctuality, customer service, innovation or all of the above? Write down your core principles and communicate them clearly in job postings, interviews and day‑to‑day operations. A strong culture attracts people who share your values and repels those who don’t. It also guides decision‑making when tough situations arise.

Hire for Attitude, Train for Skill

Technical skills are important, but you can teach a motivated worker how to use a tool or follow a process. It’s much harder to change someone’s attitude or work ethic. Look for candidates who demonstrate reliability, attention to detail, communication skills and a willingness to learn. During interviews, ask behavioural questions about how they handled past challenges. Check references carefully to verify punctuality and professionalism. Once hired, invest in training to build technical competence and instil your standards.

Create a Structured Onboarding Program

Onboarding is more than filling out paperwork. It sets the tone for an employee’s experience and contributes to long‑term retention. Develop a checklist that covers everything new hires need to know: company policies, safety protocols, tools and equipment usage, job shadowing and performance expectations. Pair new employees with experienced mentors who can answer questions and model best practices. Provide them with written SOPs and videos they can reference later. A well‑designed onboarding program reduces early turnover and accelerates productivity.

Offer Competitive Compensation and Benefits

Pay matters, especially in industries where skilled labour is in high demand. Research prevailing wages in your area and offer rates that reflect the value you expect your team to provide. Whenever possible, include benefits like health insurance contributions, retirement plans or paid time off. If you can’t match larger competitors on salary, find other ways to stand out: flexible schedules, profit‑sharing, training stipends or company‑provided tools. Show candidates a clear path for growth and pay increases based on performance.

Provide Ongoing Training and Growth Opportunities

The best workers want to grow. Offer regular training sessions on new techniques, safety practices and customer service. Pay for relevant certifications and encourage attendance at trade shows or workshops. Promote from within whenever possible and outline advancement paths (e.g., apprentice → technician → crew lead → project manager). Regular performance reviews with constructive feedback and goal setting help employees see their progress and feel invested in the company’s future.

Foster Communication and Feedback

Healthy teams communicate openly and respectfully. Hold regular meetings to discuss upcoming jobs, challenges and successes. Use tools like Slack or group texts to facilitate quick updates without endless phone calls. Encourage technicians to share ideas for improving processes or customer experience. When issues arise, address them promptly and privately. Recognise achievements publicly – a shout‑out for a job well done boosts morale and reinforces desired behaviours.

Invest in Safety and Well‑Being

In the trades, safety can literally be a matter of life and death. Provide proper personal protective equipment (PPE), enforce safety protocols and conduct regular training. Maintain your vehicles and tools to prevent accidents. Show employees that you value their well‑being by offering wellness programs, mental health resources or ergonomic equipment. A safe work environment reduces injuries, downtime and insurance costs while improving morale.

Building a winning team requires time, resources and intention. But when you assemble a group of skilled, motivated individuals who share your values and vision, the payoff is enormous: better work quality, happier clients and a business that’s positioned for sustainable growth.
